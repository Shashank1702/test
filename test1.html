अष्ट_वक्रासन

ABOUT IT

      Astavakrasana -“Asta” in Sanskrit means eight and “vakra”
      means curved or bent.

      Hence Astavakrasana means eight angle pose, one of the most
      advanced arm balance poses in yoga.

      It helps to develop stability and strengthen the wrists and arms.

       Step-1

       Begin by sitting with your feet stretched out straight and
       with palms touching the ground on either side…
       Now extend your left leg straight with the foot flexed.
       Hug your right knee
       to your chest. Now in this posture take three breaths.
       This opens up your hip.

       Step-2

       From here, place your palms to the ground. Now
       bring your right leg behind your right shoulder,
       in such a way that your right thigh rests on your right
       triceps.

       Step-3

       Draw your shoulders onto your back. On inhaling,push down
       through your hands and lift your hips and left leg off the ground.
       Hook your left ankle onto to your right ankle. While exhaling,
       bring your shoulders forward to form a 90 degree bend in your
       elbows, at the same time swinging your feet over to the right side.
       While doing so, to keep your legs firm, squeeze your right arm with
       your inner thighs.

       Step-4

       Stay firm in this position and focus on a point in the
       ground infront of you. Hold this position for 30 seconds.
       Then gently go back to original position. Rest for a few
       seconds and repeat the same by starting with the right leg.

       BENEFITS

       • It helps to strengthen the wrists, arms and shoulders.
       • Being a yoga that focuses on stability, it improves sense of balance.
       • Also abdominals and inner things are toned.
